This one starts with a question.

Introduction

Why $0 Days Matter

A $0 day interrupts autopilot.

It asks you to pause before the tap of a card or the click of a button, and to notice that every purchase begins with a story.

“I deserve this.” “It’s only a few dollars.” “I’ll save tomorrow.” “I need a little pick-me-up.”

Sometimes those stories are true. Sometimes a warm cup of coffee is exactly what your soul needs.

But sometimes, we spend without ever asking why.

A $0 day creates space for that question.

Over time, you’ll discover that this journal isn’t really about spending less. It’s about becoming more aware of the stories you tell yourself, the habits you’ve stopped noticing, and the choices that truly align with what you value.

Because every dollar you spend is also a choice about where your attention, your energy, and your life are going.
Introduction

How to Use This Journal

Each month follows the same simple rhythm:

1
Color in a calendar — tap a day, all month long.
2
Reflect at month’s end — a few honest questions about what you noticed.
3
After five months, write yourself a letter — not a summary of numbers, but of what changed.

There’s no perfect way to do this. Some months will have more green squares than others. That’s not failure — that’s information. Just keep noticing.

Rules

What Counts

A $0 Day means exactly what it says.

No money was spent today. No purchases. No payments. No exceptions.

There are no categories. No loopholes. No negotiations.

This journal doesn't separate spending into good or bad, essential or non-essential. A dollar spent is a dollar spent.

This isn't about perfection. It's about paying attention.

Rules

The Mindset

The goal isn't to collect the most $0 days. The goal is to notice how often spending has become the default — and to discover that it doesn't always have to be.

Stay curious.

This journal isn't here to judge you. It's here to help you understand yourself.

When you spend, ask yourself:

— What story was I telling myself?
— What need was I trying to meet?
— Would I make the same choice again?

Every purchase has a reason. The more curious you become, the more intentional your choices will be.

Journal

Write it Out

Use this space to write out your thoughts and review them — or write a letter to yourself.

This journal ends here. Your reflection does not. Write yourself a letter. Let it answer questions like these:

— When you started this journal, what did you expect?
— How has your relationship with spending changed?
— What do you value more now?
— What did you discover about yourself?
— What does “enough” mean to you today?
